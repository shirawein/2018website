My   Story

After graduating from Germantown Academy in 2015, I began attending Lafayette College, where I study Computer Science and Spanish as a member of the Class of 2019. I am passionate about the role of women in computing and natural language processing, combining my interest in computing and modern language, in an attempt to bridge the gap caused by language barriers. Below is a photograph that was used in advertising my TedxLaf talk in the fall of 2015, called "The Role of Women in Computing."

Experience

My activities include serving as the President for both the Association of Computing Machinery and Women in Computing. During the summer of 2016, I served as an EXCEL Research Scholar, doing computer science research focused on developing a modeling system to analyze the biological process of signaling cascades.
    Research Experience:
  • Modeling System for Signaling Cascades
  • Cost/Benefit Analysis of Standard Graph Models in Computer Network Design
  • Financial Analysis of the Planned Parenthood Federation of America
  • Statistical Analysis of the Relationship between Financial Need and Preferred Subjects Learned in a Summer Program
    Work Experience:

  • May to August 2016 EXCEL Research, Lafayette College Computer Science Department; EXCEL Research Scholar: Computer Science Research
  • May to June 2015 Bryn Mawr College, Department of Finance; Intern Reporting to the Bryn Mawr Chief Financial Officer/Treasurer
