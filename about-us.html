My   Story
After graduating from Germantown Academy in 2015, I began attending Lafayette College, where I study Computer Science and Spanish as a member of the Class of 2019. I am passionate about the role of women in computing and natural language processing, combining my interest in computing and modern language, in an attempt to bridge the gap caused by language barriers. Below is a photograph that was used in adverising my TedxLaf talk in the fall of 2015, called "The Role of Women in Computing."

Leadership   Experience

Language   Skills
My activities include serving as the President for both the Association of Computing Machinery and Women in Computing. During the summer of 2016, I served as an EXCEL Research Scholar, doing computer science research focused on developing a modeling system to analyze the biological process of signaling cascades.
My language skills include both foreign and technical languages. In addition to being fluent in Spanish, I have experience with Java, C++, Scala, R, Netlogo, Processing, Javascript, HTML and CSS, and Assembly.
